fix(schedules): disable screen suspension to restore CalendarKit native gestures

[Root Cause]
React Native's `freezeOnBlur` capability inside the Tab Navigator was aggressively suspending the component tree when users navigated to the task creation stack. Upon "thawing," the `react-native-gesture-handler` instances inside `CalendarKit` severed their native bindings, resulting in broken scroll-locks and orphaned touch coordinates (events floating while the master ScrollView incorrectly scrolled).

[Solution]
- Explicitly disabled `freezeOnBlur` in `apps/native/app/(main)/_layout.tsx` to maintain continuous PanGesture hierarchy and Reanimated listener stability across navigation transitions.
- Added production-level architectural comments detailing the strict requirement for disabled screen suspension when utilizing complex native drag-and-drop contexts. 
- Documented the `executeEventUpdate` Triple-Write atomic sequence (Convex Sync -> SQLite Cache -> Kotlin Alarm Reschedule) in `schedules.tsx` to ensure downstream workflow maintainability.

[Impact]
Calendar drag-and-drop functionality remains perfectly fluid at 60fps regardless of forward or backward stack navigation pops, with no regression in layout performance.
