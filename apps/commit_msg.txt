fix(native/blocker): resolve cyclic false-positive location blocks

Calibrated the GPS staleness threshold to align with Android's background 
location throttling logic. 

Problem:
Android OS restricts background GPS updates to roughly 20-second (20,000ms) 
intervals. Our enforcement engine had a strict 10,000ms staleness threshold. 
This caused a "logic loop" where users were incorrectly blocked for 
~10 seconds of every 20-second cycle because the app considered the 
location data "stale" before the next OS update arrived.

Solution:
- Increased `LOCATION_STALENESS_THRESHOLD_MS` from 10s to 30s.
- This threshold accounts for the 20s background polling rate plus a 
  10s hardware/processing buffer.
- Added [STALENESS CALIBRATION PROTOCOL] documentation to the Kotlin 
  source to ensure future maintainers understand the OS-level constraints.

This maintains our "Fail-Closed" security posture while eliminating 
annoying service interruptions during normal background tracking.
